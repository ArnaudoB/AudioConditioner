The bird flew away and alighted on the house of a goldsmith and began to sing: ‘My mother killed her little son; My father grieved when I was gone; My sister loved me best of all; She laid her kerchief over me, And took my bones that they might lie Underneath the juniper-tree Kywitt, Kywitt, what a beautiful bird am I!’ The goldsmith was in his workshop making a gold chain, when he heard the song of the bird on his roof.
